Mercury has no moons or rings. Its lack of atmosphere means it cannot support life as we know it, and its extreme temperatures make it inhospitable.

Mercury's orbit around the Sun is highly elliptical, meaning it has a varying distance from the Sun. It takes about 88 Earth days to complete one orbit, and its rotation period (the time it takes to spin once on its axis) is about 59 Earth days.

This means that a day on Mercury (one full rotation) is longer than a year (one full orbit around the Sun).
